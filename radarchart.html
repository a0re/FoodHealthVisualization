The radar chart below illustrates the distribution of various types of food supply and consumption in OECD countries, offering a detailed view of consumption trends. From 1995 and over the following two years, consumption patterns across all categories remained relatively stable. However, in recent years, there has been a noticeable and gradual increase in food consumption across different categories. This trend suggests a shift in dietary habits and raises concerns about potential adverse effects on health, such as increased rates of obesity, cardiovascular diseases, and other diet-related conditions. Understanding these consumption patterns is crucial for developing effective public health policies and dietary guidelines aimed at improving overall health outcomes and life expectancy in OECD nations. By examining these trends, policymakers can identify areas where interventions may be needed to promote healthier eating habits and mitigate the negative health impacts associated with increased food consumption.
